Summary

The Aurebesh alphabet is a significant feature in the "Star Wars" universe, serving as the primary script for written communication across the franchise. Originally designed by Joe Johnston for "Return of the Jedi," Aurebesh was expanded into a full alphabet by graphic designer Philip Metschan for later films. This script corresponds to the English alphabet, enabling straightforward transliterations and enhancing the visual authenticity of the series. Aurebesh adds depth to the Star Wars setting, appearing on everything from starship consoles to signage, enriching the immersive experience for fans. Being a transposition cipher, where each English letter is replaced by a corresponding Aurebesh symbol, it provides a layer of encryption to written texts in the Star Wars universe.

Don't forget to put your volume up to listen to the encrypted message... read out loud by C-3PO 😂!
